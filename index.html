Magazine

This project was created for a first-year GBDA course as a personal reflection on my growth. Entering this course, I came from a strong fine arts background, but I wasn’t fully confident in applying my ideas through design. I was comfortable creating visually expressive work, but I didn’t yet understand how to balance creativity with structure and user experience. This project became a space to reflect on where I started, what inspires my visual style, and how my perspective on design has grown throughout the semester as I learned new tools and ways of thinking.

By the end of this project, I began to see design as more than just aesthetics, but about communication and problem-solving. Exploring colour, shape, typography, and symbolism helped me understand what draws me to certain designs and how those influences shape my own work. This reflection captures my growth in confidence and shows how I’m learning to bridge my fine arts background with purposeful, user-focused design as I move forward.
